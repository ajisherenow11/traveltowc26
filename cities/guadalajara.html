Local tips for Guadalajara
🚌
Mi Macro Periférico BRT is your best route
The Mi Macro Periférico rapid bus transit system connects central Guadalajara to Zapopan and the stadium area. Combined with Mi Tren Line 3, this is the most reliable way to reach Estadio Akron on match days without getting stuck in traffic.
🚗
Uber and DiDi are safe and affordable
Both work well in Guadalajara. From central areas expect around 30 minutes to the stadium on normal days and significantly longer on match days. Book return trips in advance. Avoid street taxis.
🌡️
Pleasant climate — cooler than Mexico City
Guadalajara sits at 5,141 feet, lower than Mexico City. June weather is warm and pleasant at around 25-30°C with afternoon rains. Much more comfortable than the US southern cities. Light layers for evenings.
💧
Bottled water only
Same as all of Mexico — drink bottled or purified water only. Tap water is not safe for international visitors. Hotels provide purified water.
🥃
Birthplace of tequila and mariachi
Guadalajara is where tequila originates — the town of Tequila is just 60km away. The city is also the birthplace of mariachi music. Both are celebrated everywhere you go. Avenida Chapultepec is the best area for nightlife and bars.
🎉
Fan Festival at Plaza de la Liberación
The official FIFA Fan Festival is at Plaza de la Liberación in the historic centre, with live match broadcasts, food and cultural events. Easy walk from central hotels throughout the tournament.

Getting to Estadio Akron
🚌
Mi Macro Periférico BRT (recommended)
Rapid bus transit from central Guadalajara toward Zapopan. Most reliable option to avoid match day traffic gridlock.
🚌
Official FIFA shuttles
FIFA and city authorities will run official fan shuttles from downtown fan zones on match days. Check official channels closer to the tournament.
🚗
Uber or DiDi
About 30 minutes from central areas on normal days. Much longer on Mexico match days. Parking near Estadio Akron is very limited.
